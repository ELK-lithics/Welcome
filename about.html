About Me

My name is Emmanuel La O Kirchner. I am researcher specialising in lithic analysis with a particular focus on the Neolithic of Britain currently studying at the university of Halle-Wittenberg, Germany.

I have been investigating the Neolithic archaeology of East Hampshire since 2020, undertaking fielwalking surveys, visiting collection held by museums and much more

I am currently in the process of writing by bachelor thesis at the university of Halle-Wittenberg, where I have been a student since Ocotber 2022. My thesis is focussed on a flint scatter which I uncovered via fieldwalking, which dates primarily to the Late Neolithic. Amongst the assemblage is a high proportion of flint and chert mining picks which suggest flint mining in the locality. I argue that these picks, amongst other mining-related evidence can be used as a proxy for flint mining.
